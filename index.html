About Me

Hi, I am Sonia, a person who has done a lot of things. I like learning Central Asian languages, talking about the importance of access to clean water and safe sanitation, and working towards a better world than the one we have right now. I currently live in the beautiful southwest of Scotland with Matthew Kusner and our two senior rescue dogs, Nora Ruth and Karl T

Career

I currently work for NatureScot, an organisation that advises the Scottish Government on nature conservation.

Prior to this, I served as an education volunteer with the United States Peace Corps in Kazakhstan (2010-2011), earned a Master of Public Health (2012-2014) from the George Warren Brown School of Social Work and Public Health on a full service scholarship, worked in racial health disparities research for the Washington University in St. Louis Medical School (2013), was a refugee health specialist at the International Institute of St. Louis (2014-2015), served as the Health Programme Coordinator for the Ghana Health and Education Initiative (2016), re-trained and worked as an organic grower (2017), and spent 3 years as a research fellow at the London School of Hygiene and Tropical Medicine (2018-2021) working on drivers of antimicrobial resistance.

I was the seed saving project manager for 3 years at OmVed Gardens (2020-2023), running workshops on compost, gardening, and seed saving for a more resilient future. My team won a silver medal at the Chelsea Flower Show in 2022 for our exhibition, Saving Seed

I speak Kazakh (intermediate high), Uzbek (intermediate low) and can bumble around in present tense French

Canines

This website would not be complete without a section for Nora Ruth and Karl T, the two best dogs (all dogs are good dogs though). Both struggle with some of life's harder moments, as I do, and have taught me so much about patience and resilience. I work with, and am a strong advocate of positive methods training only. Those who choose to use negative reinforcement and use pain as part of their ''training'' are assholes and should not be trusted. A few years ago, I had the opportunity to write about Nora and Karl for Where the Leaves Fall. Here is an excerpt from that piece, that sums up who they are.

They will have known more years of neglect and violence than years without them. Karl, the gentle boy covered in scars, for years caged with a concrete floor; tentative, spinning when he is happy, with a tenor range. Nora, the big girl with a fear of people; humans are always a violent threat until months prove otherwise. A broken spine tells us of the humans she knew before us. She has galaxy eyes, playfulness, and a range of soft barks, each of which means something different, which she has taught me to understand.    Of all that I have done in my life, their trust is what I am most proud of.
